### NEW mechanical finding — the per-section 0–100 score is a SUM, not a mean

Across the uniform-fill reports, each section's "Your average score" (0–100) = **uniform value × (number of
questions in that section)**: Your Employees ×8, Company Culture ×5, Goals&Strategy ×7, Leadership Team ×4,
Operational Processes ×5, Sales&Marketing ×5, Scalability ×6, Finance&Cash ×5, Your Leadership ×10, Internal
Comm ×6 — **and each multiplier equals that section's question count.** So Esperto's per-section "score" =
**the SUM of the section's 0–10 answers** (max = 10 × question count; "average" is a misnomer). Cross-check:
CEO's Your-Employees matrix value 2.8 (0–10 avg) × 8 questions = **22.4 ≈ 22** = his reported section score.
The **CEO matrix value (0–10) = the MEAN** of the section's answers; the **section "score" (0–100) = the SUM**.
The **overall ScaleUp 0–100** is NOT a plain mean (all-5s → 47, not 50) — it weights items + folds in
ambition/past-growth + bonus points, and can exceed 100.

Overall-ScaleUp anchors from uniform fills (raw, integer): all-0s → **3**, all-3s → **28**, all-5s → **47**
(confounded: different FTE/Delegation), all-7s → **62**, all-10s → **107**. Real John CEOExec → **19**
(estimate 25, peer avg 53.1, "98% score higher").
